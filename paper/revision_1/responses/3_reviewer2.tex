\reviewerblock{Reviewer~2}{%
We thank the reviewer for acknowledging that the manuscript is well-written,
technically sound, and empirically validated, and that distinguishing semantic
from supervisory uncertainty is appealing. We take the novelty concern very
seriously and have addressed it on three fronts so that the contribution is a
\emph{principle}, not a combination of existing components.}

\cresp{The major drawback is insufficient novelty: the idea of distinguishing
semantic uncertainty from supervision uncertainty is appealing but does not
constitute a new theoretical principle or algorithmic idea. The results appear to
be based mainly on the correct combination of existing methods rather than the
development of a novel approach.}
{We address this on four fronts.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(a) A formal decoupling principle (new theory, \S X).}
We now formalize the two ambiguities as acting on \emph{different terms of the
learning objective}: supervisory ambiguity is label-side conditional entropy /
annotation noise, whereas semantic ambiguity is feature-side inter-class
mutual-information aliasing. From an information-theoretic / feature-selection
viewpoint, a single mechanism conflates two terms that should be treated
orthogonally, which \emph{necessarily} motivates SLR-C (feature/decision side)
and UTD (label/supervision side) as separate modules. This reframes the
contribution as a principle with testable predictions, not an engineering
combination.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(b) Unified vs.\ decoupled, head to head (E3, new
table).} We added a controlled comparison in which a single unified mechanism is
given the same inputs and budget as decoupled FDIL. \todo{insert result showing
decoupled $>$ unified, demonstrating the decoupling is not vacuous}.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(c) Generalization on a second dataset (E4).}
\todo{insert second-dataset result; if EMOTIC, frame it carefully as a
generalization probe rather than a same-task large-scale benchmark, per the
plan's risk note}.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(d) The two modules contribute separately and
additively (E2, threshold-free).} On mAP, UTD alone contributes \res{$+2.09$}
over the CLIP baseline ($50.98\!\to\!53.07$) and adding SLR-C contributes a
further \res{$+1.48$} ($53.07\!\to\!54.55$), for a total of \res{$+3.57$}.
Because each decoupled module yields an independent, measurable increment on a
threshold-free metric, the decoupling is empirically separable rather than a
single re-labeled mechanism.}
