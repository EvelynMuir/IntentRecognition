This viewpoint also clarifies why a strong CLIP baseline already performs well. Once visual representation is sufficiently expressive, the remaining bottleneck is no longer basic visual recognition, but the mapping from rich visual semantics to abstract intent labels. In other words, the main challenge is not merely seeing better, but deciding better under ambiguity.

\subsection{Limitations and Future Directions}

Despite the consistent gains, the framework has several limitations. First, it depends on offline text generation and external prior construction, increasing preparation cost, even though UTD adds no inference-time cost. Second, the gains shrink on weaker backbones such as ResNet101, indicating that residual refinement still relies on reasonably strong base representations. Third, richer priors are not automatically better unless their interaction with the student is carefully controlled. \revised{Fourth, per-class attribution reveals a failure mode: for a few broadly defined, overlapping categories, the residual student can resolve the local candidate set toward a competing neighbor and lose accuracy on the original class.
Fifth, the framework generalizes to an adjacent subjective task. On EMOTIC~\cite{kosti_context_2019}, privileged distillation significantly improves an image-only baseline, and where per-sample annotator agreement is available the gate activates and directs its benefit toward low-agreement samples as intended. Because EMOTIC supplies rich training-time agreement on only part of its data, the gate's decisive advantage is established on Intentonomy, whose native three-annotator training labels make it identifiable. We therefore claim framework-level generalization while scoping the gate's demonstrated gains to datasets with native agreement annotations.
}

These limitations suggest several future directions: learning source-aware prior weighting instead of uniform averaging, designing stronger students for weaker backbones, and incorporating explicit candidate-level reasoning so that textual guidance operates through confusion-pair-specific evidence resolution rather than only global distillation and local reranking.
