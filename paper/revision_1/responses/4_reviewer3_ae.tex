\reviewerblock{Reviewer~3 (Associate Editor)}{%
We thank the Associate Editor for the focused, actionable points.}

\cresp{The authors are encouraged to clearly state, in the Abstract, the
quantitative improvements achieved by FDIL compared to existing approaches.}
{Done. The Abstract now reports concrete numbers under the matched CLIP ViT-L/14
backbone and identical validation-only thresholding protocol: \res{Avg-F1 $+3.4$}
and \res{Hard-F1 $+4.9$} over the strong CLIP baseline (mean over 3 seeds),
together with \res{$+3.6$ on threshold-free mAP}, with wording emphasizing that
the gain reflects better intent discrimination rather than calibration alone.}

\cresp{In Section~3.3.1 (Semantic Prior Construction), provide a more detailed
justification for the selected scenarios, including their representativeness and
the expected outcomes in conditions not covered by the presented scenarios.}
{We expanded \S3.3.1 (Semantic Prior Construction, ``Scenario selection and
coverage'') with the selection criteria, a coverage argument over the
intent taxonomy, and the expected behavior -- graceful degradation toward the
lexical/canonical sources, the base visual logits, and the residual student --
for conditions not covered by the predefined scenarios.}

\cresp{Table~2 shows FDIL below HLEG on the Easy subset, yet
this is not discussed. Explain the possible reasons and discuss the key
observations in the table.}
{We now discuss the Easy subset explicitly (Section~\ref{sec:experiments},
difficulty-subset paragraph, and the per-class attribution in
Table~\ref{tab:per_class_attr}). With the updated 3-seed numbers, the strong CLIP
baseline ($81.72$) already \emph{exceeds} HLEG ($81.49$) on the Easy subset, and
FDIL ($81.19$) is within $0.5$ of HLEG. The Easy subset is dominated by visually
unambiguous categories where semantic-overlap modeling and uncertainty-guided
distillation offer little headroom, so FDIL trades a negligible amount of Easy
performance for large gains on the Medium ($+4.3$) and Hard ($+7.8$) subsets,
where both ambiguities are active -- precisely the regime FDIL targets. The
per-class attribution confirms that the only non-trivial regression
(\textit{CreativeUnique}) is a semantically overlapping category, not an easy
visually clear one.}
