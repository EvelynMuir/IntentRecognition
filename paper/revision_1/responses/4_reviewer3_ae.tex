\reviewerblock{Reviewer~3 (Associate Editor)}{%
We thank the Associate Editor for the focused, actionable points.}

\cresp{The authors are encouraged to clearly state, in the Abstract, the quantitative improvements achieved by the proposed FDIL method compared to existing approaches.}
{Following this recommendation, we have revised the Abstract to report the controlled gains over representative recent methods: at least \res{$+3.4$ Avg F1}, \res{$+5.7$ Hard F1}, and \res{$+3.4$ threshold-free mAP} under matched CLIP ViT-L/14 features and validation-only thresholding.
\revloc{Abstract (final sentence) and Table~\ref{tab:main_results}}
\revshot{abstract}}

\cresp{In Section~3.3.1, Semantic Prior Construction, the authors should provide a more detailed justification for the selected scenarios, including their representativeness and the expected outcomes in conditions not covered by the presented scenarios.}
{We appreciate the request for an explicit selection criterion and uncovered-case behavior. We have revised Semantic Prior Construction to state that a fixed prompt generates scenarios uniformly for every class along subjects/objects, actions, settings, atmosphere, and photographic style; inclusion targets representative visual realizations rather than exhaustive coverage. Specifically, the scenario source is normalized and averaged with lexical and canonical sources, then used only within a Top-$K$ reranking stage. For an uncovered condition, its influence therefore diminishes and prediction falls back toward the other text sources, base visual logits, and residual student rather than failing discontinuously. % 这一段需要结合正文最新版修改
\revloc{Sec.~\ref{sec:method} (Semantic Prior Construction) and Table~\ref{tab:prior_ablation}}
\revshot{scenario_prior}}

\cresp{Table~2 shows that FDIL achieves lower performance than the conventional HLEG method on the Easy subset, yet this observation is not discussed. The authors should explain the possible reasons for this result and clarify whether certain characteristics of the Easy subset contribute to the similar performance between the two methods. Further discussion of the key observations in the table is recommended.}
{This Easy-subset exception is now stated explicitly rather than absorbed into the aggregate result. The revised discussion reports that FDIL scores \res{$81.19$}, $0.30$ below HLEG, on this largely visually unambiguous subset. Specifically, the two ambiguity-targeted components have less headroom there, while FDIL's gains are larger on the Medium and Hard subsets, where semantic overlap and annotator disagreement are more pronounced. We now present this as a scope observation, not as evidence of uniform dominance. % 这一段把正文里的分析内容也再说一下
