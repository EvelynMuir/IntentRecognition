\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this work, we studied subjective visual intention recognition from a stage-aware perspective and argued that supervisory ambiguity and semantic ambiguity should be handled differently across training and inference. Based on this view, we proposed SADIR, which combines Uncertainty-guided Text Distillation (UTD) for robust learning under annotator disagreement with Semantic Local Reranking and Calibration (SLR-C) for local semantic refinement at test time.

Extensive experiments on Intentonomy showed that the proposed framework consistently improves over strong visual baselines and prior state-of-the-art methods. Overall, the results suggest that once visual representations are sufficiently strong, the key challenge of intention understanding lies in resolving subjective and semantic ambiguity rather than in extracting low-level visual features alone. We hope this stage-aware formulation provides a clearer perspective for future research on human-centered visual understanding tasks.
