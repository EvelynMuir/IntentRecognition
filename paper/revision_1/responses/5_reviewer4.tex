\cresp{Add more theoretical analysis, e.g.\ from information-theoretic or
feature-selection perspectives.}
{Added a formal analysis in \S\ref{sec:decoupling_principle}. We now motivate it
with an empirical diagnostic showing why a feature-selection / local-aliasing
view is appropriate: the visual baseline's Top-10 candidates already cover
\res{$83.9\%$} of positive labels, \res{$95.1\%$} of test images contain at least
one ground-truth intent inside that Top-10 set, and \res{$67.8\%$} of baseline
false negatives are still recoverable from the same candidate set. An oracle
restricted to this local neighborhood would reach \res{$87.4/91.3/89.7$}
Macro/Micro/Samples F1. This indicates that the core semantic failure is often
not global absence of the correct intent, but local ranking, calibration, and
separation among already retrieved, semantically adjacent candidates. Based on
this observation, we decompose the per-class excess risk into a label-side
conditional-entropy term (supervisory ambiguity, a functional of $\Pr(Y\mid X)$)
and a feature-side inter-class mutual-information aliasing term (semantic
ambiguity, a functional of the representation $\phi$), and show the two
corrections have non-interfering first-order effects -- SLR-C is a
feature-selection step that lowers the aliasing term, while UTD's agreement gate
reduces reliance on high-entropy targets. The analysis yields three testable
predictions that the experiments confirm (additive threshold-free increments,
content-dependence of the rationale teacher, and non-uniqueness of the decoupled
realization). See also Reviewer~2 comment~(a).}

\cresp{A comparative analysis of model parameters, FLOPs, or inference times is
necessary.}
{We now report exact, same-environment params/FLOPs/latency for all methods that
admit a feature-level head, including the external baselines
(Table~\ref{tab:efficiency_comparison}). Using their public source code, we
profile HLEG and IntCLIP under the same shared frozen CLIP ViT-L/14 protocol as
their ``(CLIP ViT-L/14)'' accuracy rows, counting each method's inference-active
head on a single RTX 4090. The contrast is informative: HLEG's Query2Label
transformer decoder alone costs $203.7$M params, $12.1$G FLOPs, and $2.73$ms per
image, whereas IntCLIP/DualCoOp's learnable head is only $0.02$M prompt-context
parameters ($22.0$M FLOPs, $0.11$ms) because its classifier weights are cached
frozen-text-encoder embeddings. FDIL sits between them at $2.41$M params,
$4.93$M FLOPs, and $0.47$ms. IntentMLM is the only method kept qualitative: it
runs a multimodal LLM with caption generation and retrieval (billions of
parameters, tens-to-hundreds of milliseconds per image), whose cost is dominated
by the LLM rather than a feature-level head. See E8,
Section~\ref{sec:experiments} (Efficiency Comparison).}
