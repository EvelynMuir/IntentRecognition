\reviewerblock{Reviewer~2}{%
We thank the reviewer for acknowledging that the manuscript is well-written,
technically sound, and empirically validated, and that distinguishing semantic
from supervisory uncertainty is appealing. We take the novelty concern very
seriously and have addressed it on three fronts so that the contribution is a
\emph{principle}, not a combination of existing components.}

\cresp{The major drawback is insufficient novelty: the idea of distinguishing
semantic uncertainty from supervision uncertainty is appealing but does not
constitute a new theoretical principle or algorithmic idea. The results appear to
be based mainly on the correct combination of existing methods rather than the
development of a novel approach.}
{We address this on four fronts.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(a) A formal decoupling principle (new theory,
\S\ref{sec:decoupling_principle}).} We now formalize the two ambiguities as
acting on \emph{different terms of the learning objective} (Eq.~\eqref{eq:risk_decomp}):
supervisory ambiguity is a label-side conditional-entropy term that depends only
on $\Pr(Y\mid X)$ and is irreducible by any function of the features, whereas
semantic ambiguity is a feature-side inter-class mutual-information aliasing term
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:aliasing}) that depends only on the representation $\phi$. Because
the two are functionals of different objects, we show their corrections have
non-interfering first-order effects: a reliability gate that is a function of the
agreement $\omega$ alone (UTD, label side) and a reranking/residual that is a
function of $(\phi,\text{class text})$ alone (SLR-C, feature side) act on disjoint
factors of the risk, so a single scalar-weighted mechanism that mixes both signals
must trade one ambiguity against the other while a decoupled realization need not.
This reframes the contribution as a principle with three testable predictions
(separable additive threshold-free increments; content-dependence of the teacher;
non-uniqueness of the realization), each verified in Section~\ref{sec:experiments}.
We are careful not to overclaim necessity: as prediction~(P3) and the head-to-head
comparison in~(b) show, a unified control built on a single ambiguity signal comes
within about one average-F1 point of the decoupled model, while naively fusing both
signals into one target underperforms it, so we frame decoupling as the more
interpretable, separately measurable, and independently deployable realization of the
principle -- and a modestly more accurate one -- rather than the only route to a
competitive accuracy.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(b) Unified vs.\ decoupled, head to head (E3, new
table).} We added a controlled comparison in which a single unified mechanism is
given the same inputs and budget as decoupled FDIL. A unified control that mixes
SLR-C probabilities and rationale-teacher probabilities into one joint
distillation target -- removing residual decomposition and agreement-aware
gating -- reaches only $55.66$ average F1 (3-seed mean), \emph{below} decoupled
FDIL ($57.49$; Macro $50.69\!\to\!52.13$, Hard $32.41\!\to\!35.02$, mAP
$54.60\!\to\!55.10$) and at the level of the weaker single signal. The strongest
single-signal control (SLR-C only) is closer, $0.62$ average F1 behind FDIL
($56.87$ vs.\ $57.49$) and marginally higher on mAP. We therefore state the claim
as follows: naively fusing the two ambiguity signals into one supervision target
is suboptimal, and decoupling yields a modest but consistent aggregate-accuracy
benefit on top of its primary advantages -- interpretability, separate
measurability, and independent deployability.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(c) Generalization to a second, larger dataset --
diagnosed and made precise (E4, revised).}
We substantially strengthened the EMOTIC probe. Rather than reporting a flat
``no transfer,'' we now identify \emph{why} the decomposition does not move the
standard-split numbers, and we show that, once its precondition is met, the
distillation framework generalizes to this affect domain with
\emph{statistically significant} gains.

\emph{(i) Why the standard split is uninformative for the gate.} Our
agreement-aware gating reweights the rationale-teacher term by per-sample
annotator agreement. EMOTIC, however, multi-annotates only its validation and
test splits; the \emph{training} split is single-annotator, so every training
sample has degenerate agreement $=1$ and the gated term reduces to standard
knowledge distillation \emph{by construction} (we verified the gated student is
numerically identical to the ungated one on this split). The earlier
$\pm1$-point standard-split result is therefore a property of EMOTIC's annotation
protocol -- not evidence against the mechanism -- and EMOTIC's affect labels are
otherwise well predicted by the CLIP baseline already.

\emph{(ii) A controlled study where agreement \emph{is} available.} To test the
framework where its precondition holds, we pool EMOTIC's multi-annotated
val+test instances ($10{,}613$ persons, each carrying a fractional agreement
label) and run $5\times5$ stratified cross-validation entirely inside this pool,
so label construction is consistent across folds and the gate is active on
\res{$97.7\%$} of samples (mean gate weight $0\!\to\!0.51$). Here privileged
distillation improves the image-only baseline significantly:
mAP \res{$42.57\!\to\!42.77$} ($+0.20$), Macro \res{$+0.39$}, Micro \res{$+0.65$},
and Samples-F1 \res{$+0.67$}, every metric paired-significant over $25$
seed$\times$fold runs ($p<0.01$, Table~\ref{tab:emotic_pool}). We additionally
found that in-sample teacher targets are overfit ($87$ vs.\ $66$ mAP); using
out-of-fold teacher predictions is what prevents distillation from
\emph{degrading} the student, and we adopt it throughout.

\emph{(iii) Calibrated claim.} On this pool the agreement gate \emph{matches}
standard KD rather than exceeding it (all gate-vs-KD contrasts non-significant,
$|\Delta|\!\le\!0.01$~mAP, $p>0.6$), consistent with EMOTIC's agreement being
borrowed from evaluation annotators and coarser than Intentonomy's native
three-annotator \emph{training} agreement. We therefore claim
\emph{framework-level} generalization on EMOTIC -- privileged distillation
transfers to a second, affect domain and significantly beats the baseline -- while
the gate's incremental benefit is demonstrated on Intentonomy, where rich
training-time agreement makes it identifiable. We base the core novelty argument on
the formal principle in~(a), the matched-backbone gains, the unified-vs-decoupled
comparison in~(b), and the negative controls below, and we state this scope
explicitly in the Limitations (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(d) The two modules contribute separately and
additively (E2, threshold-free).} On mAP, UTD alone contributes \res{$+2.40$}
over the CLIP baseline ($50.67\!\to\!53.07$) and adding SLR-C contributes a
further \res{$+2.03$} ($53.07\!\to\!55.10$), for a total of \res{$+4.43$}.
Because each decoupled module yields an independent, measurable increment on a
threshold-free metric, the decoupling is empirically separable rather than a
single re-labeled mechanism.}

\cresp{The VLM / text prior may be mere external-knowledge stacking.}
{We added negative-control retrains (E14, new Table~\ref{tab:negative_controls})
that corrupt one ingredient of the full pipeline at a time. The decisive control
is the \emph{shuffled rationale}, where each image keeps a teacher rationale but
it belongs to a \emph{different} image. This is the single most damaging change
we tried -- it costs \res{$7.8$ Macro F1} and \res{$7.6$ Hard F1}, i.e.\
\emph{worse than removing the teacher altogether} (w/o UTD) -- because a
mismatched rationale actively contradicts the image rather than merely
withholding information. This directly rules out the ``any external text''
explanation: the gain depends on the per-image \emph{content} of the rationale,
not on the mere presence of additional text or a calibration effect. Removing
the uncertainty gate (ungated) or flattening it to a constant (uniform gate)
costs a smaller, more mixed amount, so the agreement gate is a modest refinement
rather than the main source of value. These controls focus on the
uncertainty-aware rationale teacher because it is the load-bearing component of
UTD, which is precisely the part R2 worried might be generic external knowledge.}
