\reviewerblock{Reviewer~1}{%
We thank the reviewer for a very constructive review and for recognizing the
importance of the problem and the empirical interest of FDIL. The reviewer's
central concerns -- comparison fairness, the strength of class-wise thresholding,
dependence on offline VLM artifacts, and reproducibility -- directly shaped our
revision.}

\cresp{Please provide controlled baselines under the same backbone and thresholding
protocol.}
{We added a fair-comparison protocol (new \textbf{Thresholding-decomposition
table}) in which every in-house controllable baseline and FDIL are evaluated on
identical CLIP ViT-L/14 features, the identical train/val/test split, and an
identical validation-only threshold search, isolating the contribution of FDIL's
mechanisms from backbone strength.
\emph{Result (E2, 3-seed mean$\pm$std):} under the matched class-wise protocol,
FDIL improves over the CLIP baseline by \res{Macro-F1 $+4.07$} ($47.07\!\to\!51.14$),
\res{Avg-F1 $+3.36$} ($53.37\!\to\!56.73$), and \res{Hard-F1 $+4.93$}
($27.19\!\to\!32.12$), with the gains stable across seeds (FDIL Avg-F1 std $=0.27$).
We also re-ran representative external SOTA (HLEG / LabCR / PIP-Net / IntCLIP) on
CLIP ViT-L/14 features where feasible; \todo{E1b not yet complete -- for any
external method that cannot be ported to the same backbone, state the protocol
limitation explicitly and rely on the in-house controlled baselines as the
primary fairness evidence}.}

\cresp{The effect of class-wise thresholding is very strong; please isolate it.}
{We now isolate it over 3 seeds in a four-cell decomposition
$\{\text{CLIP baseline, FDIL}\}\times\{\text{global, class-wise threshold}\}$.
The decomposition shows three things. (i)~FDIL beats the CLIP baseline under
\emph{both} protocols -- class-wise (Avg-F1 $+3.36$, Hard $+4.93$) and global
(Avg-F1 $+2.83$, Hard $+8.45$) -- so the advantage is not produced by a
particular decision rule. (ii)~Class-wise thresholding lifts the baseline and
FDIL by \emph{comparable} amounts (Avg-F1 $+2.29$ vs.\ $+2.82$), i.e.\
calibration is a shared, method-agnostic post-processing gain. (iii)~The
\res{threshold-free mAP} confirms the same conclusion (FDIL $54.55$ vs.\ baseline
$50.98$, \res{$+3.57$}). The global-threshold F1 metrics carry large seed
variance (hard-subset std up to $\pm3.9$), so we treat class-wise F1 and mAP as
the primary metrics. We clarify that the reported baseline Avg-F1 of
$53.37\!\pm\!0.72$ is the class-wise value (mean of Macro/Micro/Samples F1),
\emph{not} mAP.

\emph{Correction note (internal):} an earlier single-seed grid (E1a) had
suggested the global-threshold gain was near-zero ($+0.84$ Avg-F1); the 3-seed
E2 result shows this was a single-seed artifact, and all ``gain is mostly
calibration'' wording has been removed.}
